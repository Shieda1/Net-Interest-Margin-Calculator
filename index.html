What is the net interest margin?
the net interest margin is a formula that is used to reveal the amount of money that a bank is earning in interest on loans compared to the amount it is paying in interest on deposits and it is considered an indicator of a bank'sprofitability and growth.
How to calculate net interest margin?
To calculate net interest margin you should use the following formula:
net interest margin = (investment inccome - interest expenses) / average earning assets
example: bank x takes a loan for $310,000 for an interest rate of 8% annualy and the bank earns $350,000 at the end of the year, what is the net interest margin?
investment income = $350,000 - $310,000 = $40,000
interest expenses = 0.08 * $310,000 = $24,800
using the interest margin formula the answer will be:
net interest margin = (investment income - interest expenses) / average earning assets
net interest margin = ($40,000 - $24,800) / $310,000
net interest margin = 4.9%
